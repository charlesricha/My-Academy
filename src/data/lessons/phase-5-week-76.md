# Week 76: Financial Management

The final week. You're making money, but can you keep it?

## 1. Separate your Finances

**Rule #1:** Open a separate bank account for your business. 
Never mix your grocery money with your client's deposit. Pay yourself a "Salary" from your business account.

## 2. Tax Planning (The 30% Rule)

When a client pays you $5,000, that is **not** your money. 
**Senior Move:** Every time you get paid, immediately move 30% into a "Tax Savings" account. You will thank yourself when tax season comes.

## 3. The "Rainy Day" Fund (Runway)

Clients pay late. Projects get cancelled. 
You should aim to have **3-6 months of expenses** in your business account at all times. This gives you the power to say "No" to bad clients.

## 4. Learning Resources (The "Why")

*   **Video:** [Freelance Taxes Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S7bW7EovrGk) - Foundations of accounting.
*   **Tool:** [YNAB (You Need A Budget)](https://www.youneedabudget.com/) - The best way to track your runway.
*   **Article:** [Managing Freelance Finances](https://www.shopify.com/blog/freelance-taxes) - A comprehensive guide.

## 5. Investing in Yourself

Your skills are your #1 asset. Set aside a budget every year for:
*   Advanced Courses.
*   New Hardware (a better monitor or faster PC).
*   Industry Conferences.
