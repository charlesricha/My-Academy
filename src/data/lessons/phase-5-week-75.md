# Week 75: Finding Clients

The "Build it and they will come" strategy does not work. You have to go find them.

## 1. The Three Circles of Networking

1.  **Inner Circle:** Friends/Family. (Where 80% of first jobs come from).
2.  **Outer Circle:** LinkedIn, Local Meetups.
3.  **Cold Circle:** People who don't know you.

## 2. Niche Down to Scale Up

If you are a "Web Developer," you have 10 million competitors. 
If you are an "IoT Expert for Sustainable Vineyards," you might be the only one. 
**Pick a niche you understand.**

## 3. Learning Resources (The "Why")

*   **Video:** [How to get Clients on LinkedIn](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f-nE2eFvHhM) - Without being "spammy."
*   **Video:** [The Cold Email Strategy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J_jI5rI-j_Q) - How to reach out to strangers effectively.
*   **Article:** [Building a Portfolio that Sells](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/how-to-build-a-portfolio-that-gets-you-hired/) - Focus on results, not just code.

## 4. Personal Branding

Your LinkedIn headline should be: **"I help [Target Market] achieve [Result] using [Technology]."**
*   *Bad:* "Senior Fullstack Developer."
*   *Good:* "Helping factory owners reduce downtime using custom IoT dashboards."

## Summary for the Assignment
1.  Draft 3 "Cold Outreach" messages for different niches.
2.  Create a "Target Client List" of 10 companies.
3.  Write a 500-word blog post explaining a technical solution you built (e.g. "How I smoothed sensor data with math").

---
**Senior Insight:** Freelancing is a "Pipeline" game. You should always be looking for your *next* client while you're working for your *current* one. If you wait until the project ends to look for work, you'll enter the "Feast or Famine" cycle. Keep the pipeline full.
