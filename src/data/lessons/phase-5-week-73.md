# Week 73: Project Pricing

You've spent 18 months becoming a technical powerhouse. But if you don't know how to price your work, you'll burn out. This week, we move from "Engineering" to "Business."

## 1. Value-Based Pricing (The Senior Secret)

Don't ask "How long will this take?" Ask "How much money will this make/save the client?"
*   If you build an e-commerce site for a company that does $1M in sales, and your site improves their conversion rate by 1%, you just made them $10,000. 
*   **Pricing isn't about your cost; it's about their gain.**

## 2. Calculating your "Minimum Viable Rate"

Before you can price projects, you need to know your "Break Even" point.
`Monthly Expenses + Taxes (30%) + Profit (20%) + Savings = Your Monthly Goal`
`Goal / 80 (Billable Hours) = Your Minimum Hourly Rate`
*   **Why 80 hours?** Because as a freelancer, 50% of your time is spent on non-paid work (meetings, emails, marketing).

## 4. The "Scope Creep" Protection

Scope creep is when a client adds "just one more small thing."
*   **The Fix:** Your proposal must include a **Detailed Scope**. 
*   "Anything not listed in this scope will be billed at my hourly rate of $150/hr."

## Summary for the Assignment
1.  Research market rates for Next.js developers.
2.  Price 3 fictional projects: Landing Page, IoT Dashboard, 3D Viewer.
3.  **Think:** Why would a client pay $5,000 for a website they could get for $500 on Fiverr? (Answer: Reliability, Security, and Business Understanding).

---
**Senior Insight:** If every client says "Yes" to your price, you are too cheap. You should aim for a 50-70% "Yes" rate. The clients who say "No" because you're too expensive are usually the ones who would have been the biggest headaches anyway. Don't compete on price; compete on value.
