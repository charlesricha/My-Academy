# Week 74: Proposals & Contracts

Proposals win the work; Contracts protect the work.

## 1. Anatomy of a Winning Proposal

Don't just send a price. Send a **Solution**.
1.  **Understand the Goal:** "You want to increase factory efficiency by 15%."
2.  **The Technical Path:** "We will use ESP32 sensors to monitor downtime in real-time."
3.  **The ROI:** "This system will pay for itself in 3 months."

## 2. The Contract: Your Legal Shield

You must have these 3 clauses:
1.  **Payment Terms:** 50% Upfront, 50% on Delivery. Never start without a deposit.
2.  **IP Ownership:** You own the code until the final invoice is paid.
3.  **Limitation of Liability:** You aren't responsible if their server dies and they lose millions.

## 4. Communication Boundaries

"I am available Mon-Fri, 9am-5pm. I respond to emails within 24 hours."
If you don't set this now, your clients will call you at 10 PM on a Saturday.

## Summary for the Assignment
1.  Draft a full Proposal for a "Smart Home Dashboard."
2.  Include a section on "Project Phases" (Design, Dev, Test, Launch).
3.  Find a "Freelance Web Developer Contract" online and highlight the clauses that protect YOU the most.

---
**Senior Pro-Tip:** A contract isn't for when things go right; it's for when things go wrong. Most clients are great, but the 1% who aren't will make you glad you have a signature on a piece of paper. Never work on a "handshake" deal.
